Understanding Fundamental Duties: Citizens' Responsibilities

Fundamental Duties, enshrined in Article 51A, represent the complementary side of citizenship - what we must DO as citizens. Initially 10 duties added by 42nd Amendment, duty 11 (parental education) was later added by 86th Amendment.

Inspired by Russian and Japanese constitutions, these duties emphasize civic responsibility, environmental protection, and national unity. While non-justiciable and lacking penalties, they form the moral foundation of Indian democracy.

Recent inclusion in school curriculum and mentions in speeches have elevated awareness. For KPSC interviews, knowledge of these duties demonstrates civic awareness and constitutional literacy.

Study Notes: Fundamental Duties Essentials

Original 10 Duties (42nd Amendment 1976)

Drafted under Indira Gandhi government, inspired by Gandhian principles and borrowed concepts from socialist nations. Aimed to complement fundamental rights with duties.

11th Duty (86th Amendment 2002)

Duty to provide opportunities for education to children aged 6-14. Added to balance Article 21A (right to education) with parental responsibility.

Judicial Perspective

Supreme Court in various cases emphasized that fundamental duties are necessary for effective governance. In P. Rajendran case, court stressed they are not enforceable but morally binding.

Citizenship Education

NCERT curriculum includes fundamental duties for classes 6-12. This has increased awareness among youth about civic responsibilities.
