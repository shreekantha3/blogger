Understanding RTI Exemptions: Section 8 Deep Dive

Section 8 of the RTI Act lists specific exemptions where information need not be provided. These exemptions must be balanced against the public interest in disclosure.

Landmark judgments like Subhash Agarwal, Secretary vs. CIC, and various CIC decisions have clarified the scope of exemptions and when public interest overrides them.

Study Notes: RTI Exemptions & Appeals

Section 8 Exemptions

National Security (a-f): Information that would prejudicially affect sovereignty, security, strategic, scientific, or economic interests.

Commercial Confidence (d): Information disclosed in commercial confidence unless public interest outweighs harm.

Personal Information (j-n): Information that could invade privacy of individuals, with exceptions for public figures.

Landmark Cases

  • Subhash Agarwal vs. UOI: CIC held that political parties are public authorities
  • CIC v. Secretary: Established public interest override principle
  • ICICI Bank case: Private banks not covered under RTI Act

Appeal Mechanisms

First Appeal to FAA within 30 days, Second Appeal to CIC/SIC. Penalties under Section 20 for non-compliance.

FAQ

Q: Can exemption override public interest?

A: No. Section 8 exemptions can be overridden if public interest outweighs harm from disclosure.

Q: Are political parties covered under RTI?

A: CIC has held they are public authorities, but Supreme Court judgment is pending.
