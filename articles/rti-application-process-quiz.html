Mastering RTI Application Process: Step-by-Step Guide

The RTI application process involves a structured procedure from filing to receiving information. Understanding this process is crucial for effective use of the RTI Act and frequently tested in competitive examinations.

From online portals to fee structures, appeal mechanisms to third-party objections, this quiz covers all practical aspects of RTI filing that aspirants must master for exam success.

Study Notes: RTI Application Essentials

Application Fees and Payments

Initial Fee: ₹10 for RTI application. Payment modes include IPO, demand draft, banker's cheque, or cash deposit.

Additional Costs: ₹5 per page of information, plus postal charges. Printed copies may have additional charges.

Fee Waiver: Persons below poverty line exempt from paying fees (BPL card required).

Appeal Process

  • First Appeal: To First Appellate Authority within 30 days of receiving order
  • Second Appeal: To CIC/SIC - no time limit but should be filed promptly
  • Third Party Notice: Given 45 days to respond if information affects them

Special Provisions

Information concerning life and liberty must be provided within 48 hours. Third-party information can be disclosed after removing exempted portions.

FAQ

Q: What is the complete process of filing RTI?

A: Write application to PIO → Pay fee → Receive response in 30 days → First appeal to FAA if unsatisfied → Second appeal to CIC/SIC.

Q: How to pay RTI application fees?

A: Through IPO, demand draft, banker's cheque, cash deposit, or online payment (varies by state). ₹10 initial + ₹5 per page.
