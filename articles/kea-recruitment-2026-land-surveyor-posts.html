Selection Process

The recruitment selection process follows a multi-stage approach:

Step 1: Written Examination (Offline OMR)

  • Paper I: General Knowledge (100 marks) - 2 hours
  • Paper II: Specific Paper (100 marks) - 2 hours
  • Total: 200 marks with negative marking

Step 2: Kannada Language Test (Qualifying)

  • Separate language test (150 marks)
  • Minimum 50 marks required to qualify
  • This test does not count towards merit

Step 3: SSLC Merit Shortlisting

  • Initial candidate shortlisting based on SSLC marks
  • Combined with written exam performance

Step 4: Document Verification

  • Verification of original educational certificates
  • SSLC and degree certificate validation

Step 5: Probation Period

  • Successful candidates join 2-year probation period
  • Must pass "Kadaya Tharabeti" examination

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the educational qualification required?

Candidates can apply with B.E./B.Tech. in any branch, OR AMIE qualification, OR B.Sc in Mathematics, Physics, Computer Science, IT, Instrumentation, Electronics & Communication, or Agriculture.

Is there an interview process?

No, there is no interview process for Land Surveyor recruitment. Selection is based on written examination and SSLC marks.

What is the Kannada language test for?

The Kannada Language Test is a qualifying test (150 marks). You need minimum 50 marks to qualify, but this does not count towards merit.

Is there negative marking?

Yes, there is negative marking in the written examination. Check official notification for marking scheme details.

What happens after selection?

Selected candidates undergo a 2-year probation period and must pass the "Kadaya Tharabeti" examination to confirm the position.
